# 110. What is CEM?

**Status:** Draft
**Depends on:** Nothing.
**This chapter answers:** What is CEM, in the simplest serious form?

---

CEM (Capability Evidence Model) is a framework for evaluating human capability through evidence instead of summaries.

Most evaluation processes — hiring, admissions, promotions, choosing a collaborator — ask someone to compress their real capability into something small enough to be judged quickly: a CV, a portfolio, a short interview. That compression often obscures or omits important evidence of capability.

CEM asks a different question: can capability be investigated instead of summarized? Rather than evaluating descriptions of capability, CEM investigates the reasoning, evidence, and development behind that capability to support better-informed human decisions.

---

## The General Problem

CEM is not, at its root, a framework about AI, recruiters, or hiring.

CEM is a framework for a more general problem: **people make decisions about other people's capabilities using incomplete evidence.**

Hiring is one instance of this problem. University admissions are another. Internal promotions are another. Finding a co-founder is another. Choosing a freelancer is another. In every case, someone with limited time has to judge someone else's capability, and the default mechanism — a summary — fails both sides for the same underlying reason.

Hiring is the first application of CEM. It is not the definition of CEM.

---

## What CEM is not

CEM is not a resume optimizer. It does not help a candidate sound more impressive.

CEM is not a keyword filter. It does not sort candidates by whether their resume contains the right words.

CEM is not a single generic AI recruiter. It does not apply one company's idea of a good candidate to every company that uses it.

---

## The shortest possible version

A résumé is a claim. CEM evaluates evidence.
