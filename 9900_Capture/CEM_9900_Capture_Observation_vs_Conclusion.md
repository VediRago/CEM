# Capture — Observation vs. Conclusion

**Likely belongs to:** `400_Model/` or `800_Ethics/` — this is a constraint that should exist before other Model chapters are written, since it governs what any AI-investigator role is allowed to conclude.

## The core discovery

CEM does not claim to know how someone thinks, feels, or what kind of person they are. It records observations and leaves interpretation to the human decision-maker.

Example given: "Candidate replied after one day" is an observation. "Candidate is thoughtful" is a conclusion CEM has no right to make on its own — that's an interpretation, and interpretation belongs to humans, not the investigation layer.

## Related sub-discoveries

- **Clarification before judgment.** When evidence is unclear, CEM asks for clarification instead of assuming weakness. This is a real behavioral constraint on the AI investigator, not just a philosophy.
- **Response speed is an observation, not a signal.** Response latency should be recorded as data but must not automatically imply capability, effort, or character.
- **Investigation is asynchronous by design.** A candidate may return hours or days later with a better explanation. Confidence in an observation should update as more evidence arrives, rather than being locked in from a first impression.
- **Clarification is not coaching.** CEM can ask a candidate to expand an unclear idea — this helps reveal evidence that already exists, and is explicitly different from helping a candidate appear stronger than their evidence supports.

## Why this matters structurally

This is not just an ethical nicety — it's a load-bearing constraint that should be written and frozen *before* other Model chapters (the five-layer architecture, the AI-investigator role, the investigation dossier) are drafted. Those later chapters need to inherit this boundary from the start, rather than being written first and revised afterward to comply with it.
