# Capture — Standardization vs. Depth

**Date:** July 3, 2026
**Raised during:** Stress-testing the core CEM concept (Rule 8)
**Belongs to (future chapters):** `25_Evaluation_Lens.md`, `28_Evidence_Requirements.md`

---

## The tension

CEM lets a candidate present evidence in an open, self-led, adaptive way — no fixed question set, no predetermined order. That's correct and shouldn't change.

But open-ended conversation risks a real fairness problem: two equally capable candidates could have very different conversations depending on how well they narrate their own work. If the AI scores the *conversation*, articulateness could get rewarded over capability.

## The resolution

This is not one problem — it's two separate layers that got collapsed into one question.

**Layer 1 — how evidence is gathered.** Stays fully open, candidate-led, adaptive. Not the risk.

**Layer 2 — how evidence is judged.** Must be standardized — not by fixing the conversation, but by fixing the Evaluation Lens: the same criteria applied consistently to whatever evidence surfaces, regardless of order or format.

Comparability comes from the judgment being fixed, not the conversation being fixed.

## Why this resolves the articulateness risk

Articulateness should affect how much evidence a candidate manages to surface. It should not affect how a given piece of evidence, once surfaced, is weighed.

This maps directly onto the existing Part II distinction: **Evidence vs. Presentation**. A more articulate candidate gets more time to reveal evidence. They should not get a better score for the same underlying evidence.

## Action for future chapters

When `25_Evaluation_Lens.md` and `28_Evidence_Requirements.md` are written, they must explicitly state:

- The conversation format is not standardized and does not need to be.
- The evaluation criteria applied afterward is standardized, fixed per company, and applied identically regardless of how the evidence was surfaced.
- Scoring must target the evidence itself (does this document show real reasoning, does this artifact actually work, does this claim hold up) — never the candidate's delivery, fluency, or conversational skill, unless fluency is itself a stated job requirement.
