# Capture — Supporting Evidence Should Be Typed, Not Mixed

**Date:** July 3, 2026
**Discovered during:** Reviewing [`130_The_Problem.md`](../100_Foundations/130_The_Problem.md) against the Chapter Blueprint
**Belongs to:** [`90_Chapter_Blueprint.md`](../000_Method/90_Chapter_Blueprint.md), Section 7 (Supporting Evidence)

---

## The gap

Section 7 of the Chapter Blueprint currently lists possible evidence types as one undifferentiated bucket:

- Research
- Observations
- Case Studies
- Experiments
- Literature
- Development History
- External Validation

In practice (see [`130_The_Problem.md`](../100_Foundations/130_The_Problem.md), Section 8), this produces a list where a personal observation, an industry trend mentioned in a trade article, and a cited research finding all sit at the same visual weight. They are not the same strength of evidence. Presenting them undifferentiated implies a chapter's evidentiary support is stronger than it actually is — the opposite of what Rule 7 (Evidence Over Opinion) requires.

## The proposed fix

Future revision of [`90_Chapter_Blueprint.md`](../000_Method/90_Chapter_Blueprint.md), Section 7, should separate evidence by type rather than list them together. A candidate structure:

- **Foundational observation** — the personal, direct experience that motivated the chapter (weakest standalone, strongest as a seed).
- **External research** — peer-reviewed or otherwise rigorously sourced findings.
- **Industry signals** — trend reports, trade articles, quotes from practitioners; directionally useful but not rigorously verified.
- **Case studies** — specific, named examples examined in depth.
- **Experiments** — anything CEM itself has directly tested.

Each piece of evidence in a chapter should be labeled by which category it belongs to, so a reader can immediately judge its weight rather than assuming uniform strength across the list.

## Status

Not applied retroactively to any existing chapter yet. This is a discovered improvement to the Blueprint itself, found through actually using it — exactly the kind of finding Rule 8 (Challenge the Framework) exists to surface. Apply when [`90_Chapter_Blueprint.md`](../000_Method/90_Chapter_Blueprint.md) is next revised.
