# 30. CEM Development Lifecycle

**Version:** 0.1
**Status:** Draft

---

# Question

How does an idea become trusted knowledge within CEM?

---

# Purpose

This document defines the complete lifecycle of a CEM idea, from its first observation to its integration into the framework.

Its purpose is to ensure that ideas are developed consistently, challenged thoroughly, documented transparently, and preserved historically.

---

# Lifecycle

```text
Observation
    ↓
Capture (if not ready)
    ↓
Discussion
    ↓
Assign / Create Chapter
    ↓
Fill Chapter Blueprint
    ↓
Reasoning
    ↓
Challenge (Rule 8)
    ↓
Assumption Testing
    ↓
Revision
    ↓
Approved
    ↓
Frozen
    ↓
Archive Previous Meaningful Version
    ↓
Update Current Chapter
    ↓
Commit
    ↓
Future Challenge?
       │
   Yes ▼
Reopen Chapter
```

---

# Principles

- Ideas begin as observations, not conclusions.
- Capture protects focus; it is temporary, not permanent.
- Every chapter answers one question.
- Every chapter follows the Chapter Blueprint.
- Assumptions must be made explicit and challenged.
- A conclusion must never exceed the reasoning that supports it.
- Previous meaningful versions are archived before replacement.
- Git records every change; History preserves important reasoning milestones.
- Frozen chapters are stable, not unquestionable. Rule 8 may reopen them when justified.

---

# Notes

This document describes the process of developing CEM itself.

It does not define the framework.

It defines how the framework earns trust over time.
